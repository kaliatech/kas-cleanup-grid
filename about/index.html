About Myself and the Project

This application was created as a Twenty Time Project. The idea for a Twenty Time Project originated in the company M3, and was later further developed by Google. The concept was to give workers of the company 20% of their work time to pursue their own personal interests. The Twenty Time Project has given us post-it notes, double sided tape, and all of the Google applications like Google Docs and Google Slides.

My name is Kergan Sanderson, and I was assigned to do a Twenty Time Project as part of my Eight Grade English Language Arts class. Given the option to do whatever I wanted, I decided that I wanted to make a difference in my community. Lately, I had noticed an abundace of litter in urban areas. I also had been wanting to develop programming skills for a while, and thought about ways that I could combine those two interests. This website and the movement that it supports is my Twenty Time Project.

The website that I ended up creating was developed using parts of Google Maps and Bootstrap 4, an open source program that other programers can use for pieces of their code. My website uses Google Maps to create a map of the areas of trash around the communities of the world. Using the application, a user can see areas where trash may need to be picked up, may need to be picked up soon, or areas that are clean and have no current litter.
